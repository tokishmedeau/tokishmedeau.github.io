About

I was born in a small town where tall white columns crowned with ornate capitals cast long shadows across the horizon, turning them into dark factory chimneys billowing plumes of smoke: white, gray, almost black, rust-colored. At night, the smoke glowed with fire, like the mouth of an awakening volcano. By day, tiny flakes of black ash drifted gently onto plaster flowers, fruit, and cornucopias heavy with clusters of grapes. Beneath enormous old trees, in lush green grass, among the fragile blossoms of sourgrass, tiny beads of mercury shimmered here and there in a thick layer of weightless gray dust beneath the bright summer sun.

Perhaps that's why Southern Gothic and magical realism have always felt less like genres to me than ways of seeing the world. They allow beauty and decay, memory and reality, faith and doubt to exist side by side, asking questions instead of offering answers.

My stories explore memory, illness, family, mortality, and the fragile boundary between what is real and what we believe to be true.

Those are the stories I set out to tell.
